Habit is a 4 step process: Cue > Craving > Response > Reward.

You can apply the Laws of Behavior Change for each step to build a very strong habit.

The Laws of Behavior Change framework provides step level strategies that you can implement here.

search Cue Bit of information that predicts a reward

Make it obvious

schedule Time

The two most common cues are a time and location.

You can use the Implementation Intention strategy to pair a habit with a given time and place.

A habit is like an appointment with yourself at a given time.

 location_on Location

And at a given place. You may skip a habit, but you wouldn't miss an appointment.

 layers Habit stack

A very strong cue is another habit you already perform.

You can use the Habit Stacking strategy to pair a new habit with a current habit.

A Habit Stack is a set of ordered habits that you perform one after another.
local_fire_department Craving Motivational force behind a habit, desire to act

Make it attractive

local_fire_department Temptation Building

To make a habit attractive you can use the Temptation Building strategy.

You pair a habit to want to achieve with a reward.

The anticipation of the reward will prompt you to action.

 psychology Conditioning Step

You can also use a Conditioning Step, it's a motivational ritual, just before a difficult habit.

 groups Tribe

The culture you live in determines which behaviors are attractive to you.

You can share your System of habits with a tribe of people like you, who praise and approve the same behavior you want to stick with.
emoji_events Reward End goal of every habit

Make it satisfying

emoji_events Reward

Making a habit satisfying increases the odd that it will be repeated.

To make a habit satisfying you can pair it with an immediate reward.

 how_to_reg Identities

To make a habit satisfying you can also pair it with a long-term reward.

Identity change is the real long-term reward for a repeated habit when your habit process focuses on who you wish to become.

You can map a habit with a set of Identities. Everytime you perform this habit, it's a vote in favour this type of person you want to become.

Make it easy

speed Gateway Habit

To make a habit easy you have to reduce friction between you and your good habits.

To reduce willpower friction you can use the Two-Minute Rule tactic defining a Gateway Habit.

A Gateway Habit is the easiest action to do that makes sure your habit will be performed. This action should take less than two minutes.

Ex : "Put running shoes" -> "Run 10 km"

 next_week Preparation Habit

To reduce friction you can prepare your environment to make future actions easier.

A Preparation Habit is something you do just after finishing your habit to prepare your next iteration.

You can even define the preparation habit as an independant habit if you don't do it right after.
